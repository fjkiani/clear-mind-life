## What You Need Before You Start

- An EHR system with a FHIR R4 endpoint (Epic, Cerner, Athena, or any FHIR-compatible system)
- Payer credentials for at least one clearinghouse (Availity, Change Healthcare, or direct payer portal access)
- Your practice's NPI number(s) and provider NPIs
- A Clerk account for authentication (we'll walk you through this)

If your EHR doesn't have a FHIR R4 endpoint, contact us before starting. We support HL7 v2 and direct database integrations for legacy systems — setup takes longer but it's doable.

## Step 1: Connect Your EHR

Go to **Settings → Integrations → EHR** and enter your FHIR R4 base URL. For Epic, this looks like `https://[your-org].epic.com/api/FHIR/R4`. For Cerner, it's `https://fhir-myrecord.cerner.com/r4/[tenant-id]`.

Click **Test Connection**. We'll attempt to read a Patient resource and a Coverage resource. If the connection succeeds, you'll see a green confirmation. If it fails, the error message will tell you exactly what's wrong — missing scope, invalid endpoint, or authentication issue.

Once connected, we'll pull your active patient list and provider roster. This takes 2–5 minutes depending on practice size.

## Step 2: Add Your Payer Credentials

Go to **Settings → Integrations → Payers**. Add your clearinghouse credentials:

- **Availity:** Enter your Availity username, password, and submitter ID
- **Change Healthcare:** Enter your trading partner ID and API credentials
- **Direct payer:** Enter the payer ID and any portal credentials

We support 900+ commercial payers. If your payer isn't in the list, use the "Add Custom Payer" option and enter the payer ID — we'll route eligibility checks through your clearinghouse.

Click **Run Test Eligibility** to verify the connection. Enter any patient's member ID and date of birth. If the 270/271 transaction completes successfully, you're connected.

## Step 3: Configure Your Practice

Go to **Settings → Practice**. Fill in:

- Practice name and Tax ID (EIN)
- Practice NPI (Type 2)
- Billing address and pay-to address
- Specialty (select all that apply — Psychiatry, Psychology, Licensed Clinical Social Work, etc.)

Then go to **Settings → Providers** and add each provider:

- Full name and credentials
- Individual NPI (Type 1)
- Specialty and taxonomy code
- Supervising provider (if applicable for supervised billing)

This information populates the 837 claim header. Get it right here and you won't have to correct it on every claim.

## Step 4: Run Your First Eligibility Check

Go to **Patients** and select any patient. Click **Check Eligibility**. The Receptionist Agent will send a real-time X12 270 transaction to the patient's payer and display the 271 response.

You'll see: coverage status, plan name, copay, deductible (met/remaining), out-of-pocket maximum, and whether mental health services require prior authorization. If the patient has a behavioral health carve-out (e.g., Optum managing mental health benefits for a UHC plan), that will be identified and the eligibility check will be routed to the correct payer.

## Day One: What to Expect

On your first day, the platform will:

- **Run eligibility checks** on all patients with upcoming appointments in the next 7 days
- **Flag any prior auth requirements** for those appointments
- **Scan your pending claims queue** (if you've connected your EHR's billing data) and identify claims with coding issues before submission

The dashboard will show you three numbers: claims ready to submit, claims flagged for review, and eligibility checks completed. Start with the flagged claims — those are the ones that would have been denied.

Your billing agent is running from the moment you connect. You don't need to configure rules or set up workflows. The coding databases are already loaded. The payer rules are already in the system.

The first week is mostly review: checking that the SOAP notes look right, confirming the ICD-10 suggestions are accurate, and getting comfortable with the approval workflow. By week two, most practices are running on autopilot.
